# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Primary: **technical recruiters and engineering managers** evaluating Ashutosh for a
full-time fullstack role. They arrive from a job application, LinkedIn, or a referral,
often skimming a shortlist under time pressure and frequently on mobile. They need to
grasp *who this person is and what they've shipped* within the first few seconds, then
be able to drill into real projects, code, and technical depth if interested.

Secondary: **peers and a general reading audience** for the blog — engineers and
curious readers who arrive at a specific post. They value clear writing and a fast,
distraction-free reading experience, and may not care about the "hire me" framing at all.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio for **Ashutosh, a fullstack developer**, built to **land a
full-time role**. It exists to convert a recruiter's or hiring manager's attention into
a credible, memorable impression and a concrete next step (view a project, read the code,
get in touch). The work — shipped projects, code craft, technical depth — is the argument;
the site is the delivery. A blog extends the surface for demonstrating thinking and voice
to a broader audience.

Success looks like: a recruiter understands Ashutosh's value in one fold, an engineer
who scrutinizes the details comes away impressed by the craft, and the site itself reads
as evidence that this person ships polished, real software.

## Brand Personality

**Calm, precise, confident.** Minimal and controlled — generous space, restrained color,
nothing shouting. The craft shows through discipline, not decoration. Voice is direct and
unhyped; it states what was built and lets the work carry the weight. The neighborhood is
Linear / Vercel: dark, high-contrast, engineered minimalism where crisp typography and
subtle, intentional motion signal developer credibility. Confidence is expressed through
restraint, not through effects.

## Anti-references

Explicitly avoid all of the following:

- **Generic AI/template portfolio** — the default look everyone ships: uniform project
  card grid, tiny uppercase tracked eyebrows above every section, gradient text,
  hero-metric stat blocks, numbered `01 / 02 / 03` section markers by reflex.
- **Overloaded / flashy** — particle backgrounds, glassmorphism everywhere, motion for
  its own sake, effects that distract from the work.
- **Corporate / soulless** — stock-photo agency-template energy that reads like a company
  page instead of a specific person.
- **Cluttered / hard to scan** — dense text, weak hierarchy, no clear path to the work.
  A recruiter must never have to hunt for the projects.

## Design Principles

1. **Show, don't tell.** The portfolio is itself the proof of craft. Every interaction
   and detail is a work sample; polish is the argument, not a claim about polish.
2. **Respect the five-second skim.** The primary visitor is time-pressed. Who Ashutosh is
   and what he's shipped must land in the first fold, on mobile, before any scrolling.
3. **Restraint is confidence.** Calm and precise beats loud. Say less, space more, and let
   a single deliberate move carry weight instead of many competing ones.
4. **Survive engineer scrutiny.** Peers will inspect the details — alignment, motion
   timing, responsiveness, how it's built. The craft has to hold up under a close look.
5. **Substance over decoration.** Motion and visual richness must serve comprehension or
   emphasis. If an effect doesn't earn its place, it doesn't ship.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG 2.1 AA**. Body text meets ≥4.5:1 contrast, large text ≥3:1 — verified,
not assumed, given the intended dark high-contrast direction. All interactive elements
are keyboard-reachable with visible focus states. Because the brand leans on "smooth"
motion, every animation ships with a `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce` alternative
(crossfade or instant). The blog is optimized for comfortable long-form reading:
65–75ch measure, strong text hierarchy, no motion that interferes with reading.
